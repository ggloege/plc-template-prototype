The Pearson business most directly impacted by the launch of the International Dual Certification will be iNaCA itself, which currently offers confers a diploma only on students who complete the higher-priced, higher-intensity full-time program. Providing a lower-cost, concurrent certification opportunity may result in cannibalization of some prospective iNaCA international enrollments. We will mitigate this risk by first rolling out the International Dual Certification only through Pearson Geos; benefit accruing to the larger Pearson business and to the Geos in particular will outweigh theoretical revenue losses for iNaCA, and may expand iNaCA’s global footprint without requiring additional iNaCA marketing or sales expense.
